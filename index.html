Why TaryagNotes exists

Most Torah software is either an infinite shelf or a search box. TaryagNotes is a workbench for structured limmud: you start from a mitzvah, climb the halachic spine, attach your own thinking as atomic notes, and build a personal, navigable map over time.

Mitzvah-native navigation

The 613 mitzvos are first-class objects: browsing, filtering, linking, and review all start there.

Finite, predictable backbone

Not a folder of files: a deliberate ladder mapped per mitzvah, optimized for depth + breadth traversal.

Atomic notes that don’t rot

Notes are indexed to mitzvos and sources, bidirectional, taggable, and always synced + backed up offline.

The halachic spine (by design)

TaryagNotes follows a specific, consistent traversal model. Each mitzvah is mapped through:

  • Chumash (source and framing)
  • Shas (sugya and foundations)
  • Mishneh Torah (systematized halacha)
  • Arba’ah Turim (practical halachic structure)
  • Responsa (case-driven application)

This creates a stable “path” per mitzvah, so breadth and depth are navigable without turning into chaos.

Atomic notes (the scholar layer)

Notes are not an afterthought. They’re an integrated system: atomic entries indexed to mitzvos, source-referenced, taggable, and bidirectionally linked so your learning becomes navigable over time.

  • Atomic notes (one idea per note) + tags + cross-references
  • Source referencing (exact anchors) + backlinks
  • Offline sync + offline backup (always current, never lost)
  • Designed to reduce visual noise and protect focus

Also included (parity + polish)

Fast full-text search for “find a quote” work, and a polished reader that’s comfortable for hours — but the differentiator is the mitzvah spine + your personal knowledge layer.
