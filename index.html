The system

A mitzvah-first map of the library.

The app is organized around the taryag itself: every mitzvah becomes a home base for sources, reader history, ha'aros, and review.

Cross-referenced mitzvos

Each mitzvah carries its source trail, related sugyos, notes, and return paths instead of sitting as a flat list item.

Natural text structure

Open seforim the way they are learned: Chumash by perek/pasuk, Bavli by daf/amud, Rambam by perek, Tur by siman.

Built for ha'aros

Search, read, connect sources, and turn the sugya into lasting notes that remain tied to the mitzvah you learned.

Reader

Move through the sugya without fighting the software.

The reader is built for long sessions: readable text, stable navigation, bookmarks, recent reading, and direct jumps from mitzvos or daily learning.

  • Bavli opens as daf/amud pages.
  • Rambam opens by perek with halacha-level segments.
  • Tur opens by section and siman, not as one giant file.
  • Related Rishonim and source paths stay close at hand.

Search

Global search across the learning library.

Search is there when you need raw discovery. The difference is what happens after: results can become part of a mitzvah path, a ha'arah, or a returnable reader location.

Full-library lookup Source-aware results Review-friendly workflow

Ha'aros

A notebook for the Torah you are building.

Notes are local, searchable, and connected to the sources they came from. They are built for real review: one idea, its sources, and the path back into the sugya.

  • Atomic ha'aros for one idea at a time.
  • Source references, tags, and backlinks.
  • Local notebook database with backup workflow.
  • Search designed for review and retrieval.
