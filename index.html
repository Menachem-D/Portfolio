Local-first Torah workspace

A desktop Beit Midrash for learning the 613 mitzvos.

TaryagNotes brings the mitzvah list, source navigation, reader, daily learning, and personal notes into one focused desktop workspace. Instead of starting from a blank search box or an endless library tree, you begin with a mitzvah and move through its sources with structure.

Why it exists

Torah learning needs more than search.

Most digital libraries are good at finding text. They are weaker at preserving a learning path: what mitzvah you were studying, which sources mattered, what you wrote, and how to return to it later. TaryagNotes is built for that work.

Start from the mitzvah

The 613 mitzvos are first-class objects, not just labels. They anchor browsing, review, source references, and notes.

Move through sources

Open core texts in their natural structure: Chumash by perek/pasuk, Bavli by daf/amud, Rambam by perek, Tur by siman.

Keep your learning

Write source-linked notes, tag ideas, return to recent reading, and keep your notebook local, searchable, and backed up.

Reader

A reader built around real seforim, not raw files.

The reader is being hardened around the actual shape of the sources: Bavli pages, Rambam chapters, Tur sections, commentaries, bookmarks, recent reading, and daily learning. The goal is simple: open the text where you expect it, read comfortably, and get back to your learning without fighting the software.

  • Bavli opens as daf/amud pages.
  • Rambam opens by perek with halacha-level segments.
  • Arba’ah Turim opens by section/siman rather than giant merged dumps.
  • Daily learning entries can jump directly into the reader.

Notes

A personal notebook that stays attached to the sugya.

TaryagNotes treats notes as part of the learning system, not a side panel you eventually abandon. Notes can be tied to mitzvos, sources, tags, and backlinks so your own thinking becomes searchable and navigable over time.

  • Atomic notes for one idea at a time.
  • Source references and bidirectional links.
  • Local notebook database with backup workflow.
  • Search designed for review, not just retrieval.
